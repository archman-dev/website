# Alerting

An alert that fires at 3 AM and turns out to be a false positive wastes an engineer's sleepâ€”and erodes trust in alerting. Alert fatigue is real: if you cry wolf too often, people stop responding. Effective alerting is hard: you need to know what to alert on (not everything), when to alert (not on temporary blips), and how to alert (actionable information, not noise).

This section covers:

- **SLO-Based Alerting**: Alert when you breach service-level objectives, not arbitrary thresholds
- **Runbooks and On-Call**: Structure incident response with runbooks and clear ownership
- **Incident Response**: Manage the lifecycle from detection to postmortem
